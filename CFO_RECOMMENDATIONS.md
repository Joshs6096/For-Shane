## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Transform is at a critical inflection point. Available cash has declined from $72.6M (January 2023) to $1.5M today — a 98% erosion. Monthly operating inflows have contracted 66% over 3.5 years (from $52M/month average to $17.9M/month). The company's core businesses — Sears/Kmart retail (essentially finished), Home Services (declining 15%/year), and the Citi credit card partnership (now $0, was $78M/year) — cannot sustain operations without either:

1. **New external capital** (TopCo equity injection, new debt)
2. **KCD licensing materialization** ($41.6M forecast FY2026 — must convert to cash)
3. **Asset sales acceleration** (targeted $37.7M FY2026, must close on schedule)
4. **Cost structure further reduction** (post-reductions saved $12.6M/year — more needed)
5. **LC collateral release** ($36.4M trapped in PNC + UBS facilities)

**The next 90 days are the highest-risk period in the company's post-bankruptcy history.** At a $1.5M available cash position, any disruption to a major inflow (a delayed HS payroll disbursement reversal, a missed KCD license payment, or a delayed asset sale close) creates immediate crisis.

This document provides actionable, data-sourced recommendations for each business unit and functional area.

## SECTION 1: HOME SERVICES (HS)

*This is the company's largest cash engine: $291M net forecast in FY2026. At a 15%/year decline rate, every year of inaction costs ~$45M in reduced cash generation.*

### Current Cash Contribution (Confirmed)

| FY2025 | $382M | ($73M) | **$309M** |
| FY2026 forecast | $360M | ($69M) | **$291M** |

### Immediate Recommendations

**1. Sell the IHR contract book — initiate process NOW**  
The IHR (In-Home Repair) contract portfolio represents a declining annuity currently generating ~$20–25M/month. At a $300M annualized pace, even a 0.25x revenue multiple = $75M one-time. Strategic buyers: Frontdoor (American Home Shield parent), Liberty Home Guard, AFC Home Club, Sears Protect buyer. The longer Transform waits, the smaller and less valuable the book becomes.  
*Recommendation: Engage one investment bank within 30 days to run a quiet sale process for the IHR contract portfolio. Target term sheet by Q3 2026.*

**2. Convert Assurant profit share to monthly payments**  
The quarterly Assurant profit share ($2.2M most recent quarter) creates cash timing uncertainty. Negotiate conversion to monthly payments. This improves cash predictability without reducing the total amount. Request: $0.73M/month instead of $2.2M/quarter.

**3. Accelerate Sears Protect subscriber acquisition**  
Sears Protect is the ONLY sub-segment showing growth: $4.8M/month (Jul 2024) → $6.9M/month (May 2025). This is a prepaid recurring revenue product with strong margin characteristics. Allocate $0.5–1.0M in digital marketing to consumer win-back from American Home Shield and Cinch. Each additional $1M in annual premium = ~$83K/month in recurring inflow.

**4. Respond to CCHS B2B termination immediately**  
Cinch (Cross Country Home Services) is terminating its field service agreement with HS in May 2026. This eliminates ~$0.6M/month. Offset options: (a) Redirect Cinch technician capacity to Sears Protect expansion; (b) Approach other home warranty carriers (Cinch competitors) for a new B2B capacity agreement.

**5. Reduce HS payroll proportional to revenue decline**  
HS payroll: $11.8M/month (Apr 2026) on ~$24–30M/month in HS inflows. This implies 39–49% payroll/revenue ratio. As the IHR book declines, payroll must decline in lockstep. Each 10% reduction in HS headcount saves ~$14M annually. The post-reductions event cut HS payroll by only $4M/year — more is required.

**6. Exit Hello Super or monetize it**  
Hello Super (Transform's own branded home warranty product) is growing ($0.17M → $0.53M/month) but is tiny relative to operating costs. At $0.53M/month revenue with full tech, marketing, and claims overhead, it may be cash-flow negative. Either scale it aggressively (requires capital) or sell it to an established HW player. At current trajectory with ~3,500–5,000 contracts, book value = $2–5M.

## SECTION 3: RETAIL (SEARS & KMART STORES)

*Terminal wind-down in progress. The question is not whether to exit, but how to maximize remaining value.*

### Cash Position
| Year | Sears Stores | Kmart Stores | Combined |
|------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| FY2024 | $52.2M | $113.2M | $165.4M |
| FY2025 | $25.7M | $89.7M | $115.4M |
| FY2026 forecast | $15.3M | $83.1M | $98.4M |

### Recommendations

**1. Prioritize Kmart over Sears in any remaining allocation of resources**  
Kmart has declined only 61.5% vs. Sears' 94.2% decline. Kmart stores generate $2.4M/month in inflows vs. Sears' $0.34M/month (last 12 months). Kmart's relative outperformance suggests either: (a) better real estate/demographic positioning, or (b) Puerto Rico/USVI/Guam territory Kmarts are disproportionately strong. Either way, Kmart deserves disproportionate cost structure support.

**2. Monetize remaining real estate in store locations aggressively**  
Each Sears/Kmart store closure should be accompanied by a property disposition. The Tenant Income stream ($7M/year and declining) represents the residual value extraction from stores already closed. Accelerate the lease renegotiation/sublease program for any remaining owned properties.

**3. Territory stores (Puerto Rico/USVI/Guam) may merit separate strategic treatment**  
The Territory stores contribute meaningful ongoing revenue (Hato Rey $158–164K/month, St. Croix $63–79K/month, St. Thomas $47–58K/month in lease income alone, implying significant retail operations). These may be better operated by a local partner under a franchise/license model rather than direct management from the mainland.

**4. No new Sears investment**  
Sears Stores inflows have declined 94.2% over 3.5 years. The brand is functionally dead as a retail concept in the US. Any capital allocated to Sears retail operations has essentially no return. All remaining Sears-related spending should be managed down to zero.
